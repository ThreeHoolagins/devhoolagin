# Why no more layoff diaries?
So initially I had planned to do this either month by month or week by week- but I was already advancing with Checkr quickly by the end of January, and frankly just had other stuff I wanted to do.
As mentioned in the last one of these, I thought I might be out of work for months, and I thought this could be a cool format by which to post my experience and do a week to week metrics on my jobs applied, lessons I was learning, etc. So, in Leu of doing it the way I meant, lemme share the whole process experience!
Week 1 was frankly crazy. I structured a strict ish routine for myself that included time to work on home projects, time to do 10 applications a day, time to workout since I lacked any real excuses, and time to spend with my wife because the majority of our lives together I will have full time work and it’s an important opportunity to spend together.
This fell off around a week in, as I got invited to interviews, and completely shifted gears to studying for each and every interview. By the end of January I had taken my final round of technical interviews with checkr and frankly felt really good about them. So at this point forward I reach a bunch about offer stage and the hiring manager interview, and enjoyed my time being unemployed, pulling crazy hours on a new modded Minecraft server with my friends, and just took time to spend with my wife.
