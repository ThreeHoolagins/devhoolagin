I was born in 2001, and grew up with the internet. As my GenZers may note, this has both been a great boon to experience the internet in its less commodified state, and get to see some of the genuine passion that was poured into it when I was young, but is also a great curse, to grow up attached to the internet, making parasocial friends on youtube, have a smartphone in my pocket from age 12, likely learning from me and curating a world of distractions before I was old enough to drive.

As of the past 2-3 years I’ve been getting this growing feeling within me, fantasies of casting my phone away from me, into a lake, onto hard concrete, something that won't survive in a fit of taking over my life. But I don’t. And the fantasy lives on.

I think this comes from the phone (I have an iphone 13) being far too useful, and far too expected of us. I’ve seen the dumb phone and flip phone videos, and thought deeply about them, how I could use an apple watch ultra or a samsung flip phone, anything to get back to a time before I was addicted to being distracted.  To be honest, that’s been part of my motivation to read and to write, get away from this youtube in the background, work/shorts/video game in the primary focus, maybe even in vc.

Now I know that these things are choices, reader, my uncle called me out on this this past weekend as of time of writing. But I think it’s a few levels deeper than this; 

# Not so simple as to simply cast my phone into a lake
I grew up in an era where being an adult included getting back to people relatively quickly via text, or always being available to pick up a phone call. I managed this better when I had less going on and worked in contexts where I was expected to be off my phone the whole time; like working for panda express in the kitchen or walmart in the electronics section. I however feel an immense pressure to be available at all times, and it’s a little exhausting to be honest. Even in my own household, I feel there is a quiet expectation that we be able to respond to each other to save a trip up or down the stairs, catch an interesting or funny short, remnants of our long distance life.
Now reader, I do encourage you to not get me wrong here, I love my wife, I love our text conversations, I just don’t love the permanent anxiety of missing a text or not hearing a buzz or not being aware of the time.
I also don’t want to be a green bubble (android heads I know this is an annoying gripe), and I feel I need a smartphone as my rent is paid through an app, my authenticators for work are apps, so many things I need in my life are goddamn apps, and to be frank, I’m sick of it.
Now I get the magic of having your phone be a notepad, a camera, a cellphone, a video conference device, your bank management tool, your personal authenticator, etc etc, and sometimes I really appreciate it. I wrote Blog 3 in bed at 4 in the morning. I just wish that having one of these devices and being subject to algorithms and social pressure to response in short amounts of time weren’t there.


# Getting lost again
I got lost this weekend playing through Inscription, a great game by the way. My wife was busy, it was late and my ability to stay on top of things was slipping, and I was putting off going to bed. I got lost in that game for 2-3 hours, which I absolutely cherish. I don’t often get lost in things, constantly running through my head my lists of things that need done, groceries that need to be shopped, what I will eat for dinner each day, etc, etc, etc. I want more of this in my life. Lost in a book, lost in a game, hell even lost deep in work I find engaging. Hell, I can get lost in writing these. I want to be lost in wonder, interest, not just the zombified glazing over of playing league in vc with your friends longer than you should or some nights even want to because that’s what you do, lost in the motions of life. That type of lost I don’t want.
