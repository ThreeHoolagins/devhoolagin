Welcome reader to a fun one! This last month I attended xxx’s micro jam 52! You can check out the game here, but I’m gonna walk though my experience and talk about it!

The setup
So one of my New Year’s resolutions this year was to attend a game jam. I have a soft spot in my heart for the hackathon I went to in college and have really been looking forward to doing another for a while. I also saw both of Sebastian’s videos on this and hot damn is that cool!
From this experience, I’ve been trying to get some of my friends to do a “Minecraft mod jam” but frankly none of them are interested enough in it besides maybe one of them, and it’s just kinda a flop so whatevs! Not everyone’s life revolves around code like me .-.

Looking for a jam
So here’s the deal- I wanted a few things from my jam most of which I ended up getting!
I want to sign up for a jam that will happen soon (was looking in December)
I want to attend the jam Sebastian has done because it’s sick
Didn’t do this for my first jam, as it’s in like October (lol wtf)
I want the jam to be short, like 24 hour short if possible
I need it to be a weekend. At time of looking for a Jam, I had a full time job.
Some ranking
This is just for fun for me! Read all the way to see my placement!
And with all of these, and my searching both on Google and eventually landing on searching on itch.io, I found it!

Image of the jam

Here’s how the theming goes for this jam series: they post the general jam theme, and a secondary theme, or constraint, that you haft to incorporate, announced at jam start.

Day 1
First day started on January 23rd, at 6pm! I had my mother in law over for a movie that night, so I didn’t get going until about 9pm~10pm. As we were wrapping up, I checked my phone and saw out theme: constantly overworked.
I quickly hopped on discord and invited some of my friends in for the ideation stage. I had been telling them I was gonna stream the full weekend, whenever I was working on the project.
I eventually landed on the idea of clearing snow, as an intensifying snow storm eventually made it impossible to do so, and also decided on a temperature mechanic, as constantly overworked means to me you are juggling things until it all falls apart.

I also decided to work in game maker 2, as I already had it downloaded, was curious to learn it more, and it was one of the prize categories (was hoping for a pity 3rd lol).
So we got off to work! With project name “overblown” I made my first project, tackling menus, player movement with temp assests, and some basic temp features that night. I ended up starting to make the snow mechanic, but I hated it and didn’t know what I was doing, so I ditched it. Quick demo of end of day 1, in gif form!

Day 2
Day 2 started late in the morning with me scrapping the entire day 1 project. I was learning some stuff, and there was value in that, but nothing was organized on purpose so I could learn, and adjusting things was,,, cumbersome. In addition, I added snow particles, shop menu, and worked with my good friends Braden and Stephanie to get the menuing art, and the main game music.

I had some other stuff going on this day, and to be honest it was a big work day for me, testing out particle systems, updating assests and feel for the game, and trying to prototype and learn how to do my dammed snow system, and a pause menu which for some reason was impossible.

Late that night I ended for the day, with a basic version without the complete character assests which Stephanie had finished but I had not put in there, and a snow pile mechanic, which technically worked, but I hated.

Day 3
With a full nights rest and some time alone in the morning, I finally figured out how to separate the instance variables from each other, and created a spawner to spawn the snow in instead of having pre-placed piles. I did want to add multiple types of clearing snow, but I never ended up getting there sadly.

The last day was cleanup, as I had offered to make the family dinner for the week, which meant I needed to be done with my jam around 2pm~3pm, and when waking up around 11, I had to prioritize (look at my list below!)

Priority list image or just pasted in code block

Final thoughts
But finally, though crappy and missing several features I wanted, we had it! I had just finished my first real game ever, with credits, a highscore, and everything! If you want to play, go to websitelinj to play for yourself!

I didn’t end up using ai for this project, as to be honest I am still learning a lot about how these games programs work on a fundamental level.

Takeaways
Do more game jams!
The time constraint mixed with unknown theme is awesome for actually making something!
Draw cycles and game step cycles
I had no idea how this stuff worked before, and now that I know more I think I can make this type of thing easier.
Write out a list of features and order it, and spend more time thinking about dependencies
A shop and money system got gutted because I didn’t know how to draw the text effectively and I didn’t make things quite flexible enough to add some easy items.
Vibes are everything
When reviewing other submissions, I was surprised by how much I cared about game feel and theming. A game could look genuinely bad but if the mechanics, player speed, and other things felt good, then the game normally got better scores from me.

Conclusion 
More time constrained creative fun!! I want to do a Minecraft mod something and I’ve got an idea, and I’m excited to be part of the GMTK game jam here in the fall. Looking forward to it!
